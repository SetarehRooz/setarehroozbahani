About

Multidisciplinary architect bridging architecture with digital and design technologies. During my master’s studies, I developed a strong interest in computational design thinking and digital tools such as 3D scanning, photogrammetry, and data-driven integration in physical space, particularly as methods for understanding and informing spatial decision-making.

With more than five years of professional experience in architectural practice, I gained valuable insight into construction techniques and detailing, while investigating how digital workflows can connect practice with emerging research fields, including artificial intelligence.

My investigative and analytical approach allows me not only to deliver architectural tasks using appropriate tools, but also to explore how computational methods can support reasoning, generate knowledge, and optimize architectural processes. I am particularly interested in how data-driven systems may reshape design methodologies and architectural thinking.

In parallel, I maintain an interest in speculative drawing and investigative spatial practices, including approaches inspired by forensic architecture. I explore the computational production of images and drawings not only as representation, but as analytical tools for inquiry, interpretation, and spatial reasoning beyond the built object.

I am particularly motivated to pursue research (PhD) within research-driven practice that connects spatial design, data, and machine intelligence.
